Extracellular Vesicles Derived from VEGF mRNA-Engineered Mesenchymal Stem Cells Promote Endothelial Cell Survival.

Authors: Zhang C, Huang P, Pak M, Korchak JA, Zubair AC Journal: Cells (2026) PMID: 42041584 DOI: 10.3390/cells15080717 Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42041584/ Scraped: 2026-04-28

Abstract

Extracellular vesicles derived from mesenchymal stem cells (MSC-EVs) exhibit great therapeutic potential in ischemia-associated conditions and diseases such as myocardial infarction, ischemic stroke, and wound healing. Enhancing the therapeutic efficacy of MSC-EVs could advance their clinical application. Diverse cargos (proteins, mRNA, microRNA, etc.) in MSC-EVs contribute to the therapeutic effects in various diseases. Vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF) is one of the primary driving molecules in promoting angiogenesis and protecting endothelial cells lining blood vessels from apoptosis.
