Three-year changes in renal function after switching from injectable GLP-1 receptor agonists to oral semaglutide in Japanese patients with type 2 diabetes: a retrospective cohort study.

Authors: Takashima S, Terui K, Yamada F, Shimotomai T Journal: Diabetol Int (2026) PMID: 42078492 DOI: 10.1007/s13340-026-00903-8 Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42078492/ Scraped: 2026-05-05

Abstract

While the FLOW trial established the renoprotective effects of subcutaneous semaglutide, real-world evidence regarding the long-term renal effects of therapeutic switching from injectable glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1RAs) to oral semaglutide remains limited. We evaluated long-term changes in renal function following this therapeutic switch. This retrospective cohort study included 36 patients with type 2 diabetes (T2D) who were switched from liraglutide or dulaglutide to oral semaglutide (7 mg/day). Changes in the urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) and annual estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) slope were compared between the 3-year periods immediately before and after the switch. These findings suggest potential long-term renal benefits of oral semaglutide in routine clinical practice.
