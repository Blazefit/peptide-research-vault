🧪 Peptide Assessment Protocol

LIVING DOCUMENT — This framework will evolve as we refine the intake process, add new biomarkers, and validate routing accuracy against real-world outcomes.

Purpose

A systematic approach to determine which peptides (if any) are appropriate for an individual based on objective biomarkers, subjective symptomatology, and stated goals. This is not a prescription — it is a decision-support framework for educated self-experimentation.

📋 Section 1: Intake Questionnaire

Purpose: Capture subjective state + goals before seeing lab numbers.

A. Demographics

  • Age
  • Sex
  • Height / Weight / Body Composition Estimate
  • Training History (years, modality, weekly volume)
  • Diet Pattern (omnivore / keto / carnivore / plant-based / fasting frequency)
  • Sleep Average (hours/night, quality 1-10)
  • Alcohol / Recreational Drug Use
  • Current Medications / Supplements
  • Previous PED / Peptide Experience

B. Goal Prioritization (Rank Top 3)

  1. Fat Loss / Body Recomposition
  2. Muscle Gain / Strength
  3. Injury Healing / Recovery
  4. Sleep / Energy / Mood
  5. Longevity / Anti-Aging
  6. Sexual Function
  7. Cognitive Function
  8. Gut Health
  9. Immune Support
  10. Skin / Cosmetic

C. Symptom Cluster Scoring (1-10, 10 = severe)

Domain Symptoms to Score
Energy Morning fatigue, afternoon crash, need for caffeine
Sleep Time to fall asleep, wakeups, morning refreshed?
Recovery DOMS duration, injury frequency, illness frequency
Mood Anxiety, depression, irritability, motivation
Cognition Brain fog, focus, memory, verbal fluency
Body Comp Stubborn fat, muscle loss, water retention
Sexual Libido, erectile function, orgasm quality
Gut Bloating, reflux, stool consistency, food intolerances
Inflammation Joint pain, skin issues, allergies, headaches

D. Lifestyle Constraints

  • Budget ceiling (monthly)
  • Needle comfort (oral-only preference?)
  • Injection frequency tolerance (daily vs 2-3x/week vs weekly)
  • Willingness to monitor / re-test labs

🧠 Section 5: Contraindications & Red Flags

Absolute Contraindications

  • Active cancer (most peptides accelerate cell proliferation)
  • Pregnancy or trying to conceive
  • Severe kidney disease (eGFR < 30)
  • Active infection without diagnosis
  • Uncontrolled thyroid disease

Relative Contraindications (Proceed with Caution)

  • History of melanoma (avoid Melanotan II)
  • History of pancreatitis (avoid GLP-1 peptides)
  • Gallbladder disease (GLP-1 caution)
  • MTHFR polymorphism (avoid synthetic folate, optimize methylation first)
  • Autoimmune disease (layer immunomodulatory peptides carefully)

Drug Interactions to Watch

  • GLP-1 peptides + Insulin/SU — hypoglycemia risk
  • GH peptides + Corticosteroids — blunted GH response
  • BPC-157 + SSRIs / MAOIs — theoretical serotonin interaction (rare)
  • TB-500 + Anticoagulants — theoretical bleeding risk (unproven)

📊 Section 6: Scoring Rubric (v0.1)

Purpose: Quantify candidacy for peptide intervention.

Scoring Categories

Category Max Points Scoring Criteria
Lab Severity 30 Number of out-of-range markers
Symptom Severity 25 Average symptom cluster score
Goal Alignment 20 How well peptide class matches stated goals
Lifestyle Readiness 15 Sleep, diet, training consistency
Monitoring Capacity 10 Willingness to test, log, adjust

Interpretation

  • 80-100: Strong candidate — proceed with targeted protocol
  • 60-79: Moderate candidate — address foundational issues first, then layer peptides
  • 40-59: Weak candidate — optimize sleep, nutrition, training for 8-12 weeks, re-assess
  • < 40: Not a candidate — lifestyle interventions only until score improves

🛠️ Section 7: Implementation Notes

Lab Ordering

  • DirectLabs or Ulta Labs — self-order, no doctor required
  • Quest / Labcorp — through telehealth (Hone, Marek, etc.)
  • Cost estimate: Tier 1 = $200-400; Tier 1 + Tier 2 = $500-800

DEXA Scan Locations

  • Search "DEXA scan near me" — many universities, sports medicine clinics, or private facilities
  • Cost: $50-150 per scan

CGM Options

  • Freestyle Libre 3 — $40-80/month (OTC in US)
  • Dexcom G7 — requires prescription
