⚠️ Key Cautions

  1. BPC-157 — Not FDA-approved; injection site risk; gut-healing claims largely anecdotal
  2. GHK-Cu — Quality varies wildly; verify with HPLC; Amino Asylum specifically flagged
  3. NAD+ — Oral bioavailability poor; IV/sublingual expensive; NMN/NR may be better options
  4. HGH fragments (AOD, 176-191) — Mixed clinical evidence; fat loss claims overstated
  5. GHRH analogs — Require refrigeration; reconstitution technique matters
  6. Tirzepatide — GI side effects common; thyroid C-cell tumor risk (rodent studies); pancreatitis
  7. Retatrutide — Not FDA-approved (as of 2025); clinical trial use only
  8. Anavar — Hepatotoxic; lipid-suppressing; schedule III controlled substance
  9. Proviron — Lipid effects; check lipid panel; 25–50mg/day typical
  10. AI use — Suppressing E2 too aggressively harmful; bone health, cardiovascular risk
  11. DIY TRT — Blood work required (hematocrit, lipids, E2, liver/kidney); clinics recommended if no experience
