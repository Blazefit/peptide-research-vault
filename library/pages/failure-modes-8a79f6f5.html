Failure Modes — Peptide Pathways for Bipolar Mania Stabilization

Recurring Breakpoints

  1. Antidepressant ≠ anti-manic - Depression-oriented peptide ideas can worsen bipolar instability if they increase activation, sleep loss, or neuroplasticity without guardrails.

  2. Orexin agonism is high-risk - Orexin/hypocretin is wakefulness-promoting. For mania stabilization, agonism is initially a hard-risk direction.

  3. Sleep improvement must not be sedation-only - A candidate that merely sedates without reducing mania-like destabilization is not a clean anti-manic mechanism.

  4. NPY evidence is adjacent, not directly bipolar-specific - NPY has strong stress-resilience/anxiety literature, but weak direct bipolar mania evidence. It must be tested through stress/sleep/arousal models.

  5. BDNF/TrkB is quarantined - Neuroplasticity-enhancing peptides may help depression biology but fail mania stabilization criteria unless constrained.

  6. No human-use claims - Severe bipolar disorder requires clinical care. This loop is target-selection and assay planning only.
