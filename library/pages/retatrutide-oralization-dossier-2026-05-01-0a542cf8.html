Retatrutide Oralization Dossier — 3 Iteration Loop

Research-only note: This dossier is for scientific exploration of peptide oral-delivery feasibility. It is not clinical guidance, dosing guidance, compounding guidance, or a self-experimentation plan.

Executive Summary

The oral semaglutide breakthrough was not mainly a new semaglutide molecule; it was a co-formulated gastric delivery system: semaglutide + SNAC / salcaprozate sodium. SNAC creates a local gastric microenvironment that protects semaglutide from degradation and transiently enables transcellular stomach absorption.

The closest first-pass strategy for retatrutide is therefore not to redesign retatrutide immediately. The first program should ask:

Can retatrutide / LY3437943 be absorbed through a SNAC-like gastric delivery window while remaining intact and preserving GLP-1R / GIPR / GCGR activity balance?

Initial feasibility: possible but materially harder than semaglutide because retatrutide is larger, multi-receptor balanced, and has no approved oral precedent.

1.3 Iteration 1 conclusion

Retatrutide is only ~15% heavier than semaglutide by molecular weight, but that difference may be disproportionately important for gastric epithelial transport. The bigger issue is not just size; it is whether retatrutide’s conformation, lipidation, aggregation state, and tri-receptor activity survive a SNAC-like gastric formulation environment.

Iteration 1 decision: move forward with a formulation-first hypothesis, not sequence redesign yet.

2.3 Key external signal from literature

PMID 38070657 — Development and evaluation of C10 and SNAC erodible tablets for gastric delivery of a GIP/GLP1 peptide in monkeys.

This is highly relevant because it directly tests SNAC and C10 gastric delivery beyond semaglutide, using a GIP/GLP-1 dual agonist peptide in monkeys. That makes it a better analog for retatrutide than semaglutide alone, although retatrutide adds GCGR activity and has its own scaffold.

2.4 Iteration 2 conclusion

The top research track should be:

  1. SNAC baseline
  2. C10 comparator
  3. SNAC analog / salcaprozate-like screen
  4. Combination with release-rate optimization

Avoid chemical retatrutide redesign until formulation-first testing shows why the native molecule fails.

3.5 Iteration 3 conclusion

The next concrete research step is not molecule redesign. It is a comparative formulation/stability screen:

  1. Retatrutide alone
  2. Retatrutide + SNAC
  3. Retatrutide + C10
  4. Retatrutide + SNAC analog candidates
  5. Semaglutide + SNAC as positive control
  6. Scrambled/inactive peptide as negative analytical control where relevant

Primary readouts:

  • intact peptide recovery
  • aggregation state
  • gastric permeability
  • epithelial safety
  • preserved GLP-1R/GIPR/GCGR activity ratio

Final Recommendation After Three Loops

Best near-term hypothesis

Reta-O1: native retatrutide + SNAC-like gastric delivery is the correct first experiment because it directly tests whether the semaglutide/Rybelsus playbook transfers.

Best parallel hypothesis

Reta-O3: SNAC analog panel should run in parallel because retatrutide’s larger size and different lipidated scaffold may require enhancer optimization.

What not to do first

Do not start by modifying retatrutide’s sequence or lipid linker. That risks breaking tri-agonist pharmacology before we even know whether formulation alone can work.
