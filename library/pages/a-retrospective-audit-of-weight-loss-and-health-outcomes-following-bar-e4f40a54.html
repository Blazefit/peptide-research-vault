A retrospective audit of weight loss and health outcomes following bariatric surgery at a tertiary public hospital.

Authors: Lee JP, Bahamdan A, Franklin J, Martin D, Taylor C et al. Journal: Obes Res Clin Pract (2026) PMID: 42142983 DOI: 10.1016/j.orcp.2026.05.003 Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42142983/ Scraped: 2026-05-18

Abstract

To assess longterm weight and comorbidity outcomes following bariatric surgery at a publicly funded obesity service. Retrospective cohort study. Cohort of 203 individuals who underwent bariatric surgery between 1 October 2009 and 1 June 2024 were followed for 1-10 years postoperatively. Major obesity-related comorbidities assessed included type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM), dyslipidaemia, hypertension, obstructive sleep apnoea (OSA), arthritis and arthralgia, and depression and anxiety. Percentage change in weight over time and change in prevalence of obesity-related comorbidities. At baseline the mean age was 50.4 years (SD, 11 years), mean weight 134.6 kg (SD, 30.3 kg), mean BMI 49.3 kg/m Bariatric surgery in a publicly funded service was shown to have long-term efficacy, with weight loss maintained to 10 years follow-up and a reduction in the incidence of four major obesity-related comorbidities after 5 years.
