HGH — Overview

Human Growth Hormone (HGH, somatropin) is the direct exogenous form of growth hormone. It differs from GH secretagogues such as CJC-1295 — Overview, Ipamorelin — Overview, and Sermorelin — Overview, which stimulate the body to release more endogenous GH.

Clinic framing: separate legitimate medical GH-deficiency care from supraphysiologic bodybuilding/PED use. For member education, keep this labs-first and risk-aware.

Core Concepts

  • Direct hormone replacement/supraphysiologic hormone: HGH adds growth hormone directly rather than stimulating release.
  • IGF-1 pathway: many downstream growth/anabolic effects are mediated through IGF-1.
  • Body composition: GH can increase lipolysis and alter fat distribution, but may worsen glucose control.
  • Connective tissue: often discussed for tendon/ligament support, but the clinical/performance evidence needs careful review.
  • Not TRT: TRT targets androgen deficiency; HGH targets GH/IGF-1 axis. They interact in enhanced/bodybuilding contexts but are different medical domains.

Labs-First Assessment Topics

Potential discussion/lab markers before GH-axis conversations:

  • IGF-1
  • Fasting glucose, fasting insulin, A1c
  • CMP, kidney/liver markers
  • Lipids
  • Thyroid panel: TSH, free T4, free T3
  • Testosterone/free testosterone, SHBG, estradiol, LH/FSH where relevant
  • Blood pressure, sleep apnea risk, edema/water retention history
  • Cancer history and other contraindication screening through a clinician

HGH vs GH Secretagogues

Category Examples Conceptual difference
Direct HGH Somatropin / HGH Adds GH directly; strongest intervention; greater monitoring burden.
GHRH analogs Sermorelin, CJC-1295, Tesamorelin Stimulate pituitary GH release through GHRH pathway.
GHRP/ghrelin mimetics Ipamorelin, Hexarelin Stimulate GH pulses through ghrelin/GHRP pathway.
Combination stacks CJC + Ipamorelin Often discussed as a secretagogue stack to increase endogenous pulsatile GH.

Safety Notes

  • HGH can worsen insulin resistance/glucose control.
  • Water retention/edema and carpal tunnel-like symptoms are common practical concerns.
  • Thyroid-axis changes are commonly discussed in PED contexts but need clinician interpretation.
  • Long-term supraphysiologic use raises concern for tissue/organ growth and other risks.
  • Source quality matters; unregulated HGH may be fake, underdosed, contaminated, or mislabeled.
