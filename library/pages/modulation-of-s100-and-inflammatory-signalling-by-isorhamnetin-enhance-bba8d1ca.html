Modulation of S100β and Inflammatory Signalling by Isorhamnetin Enhances Peripheral Nerve Regeneration.

Authors: Tehreem A, Iftikhar A, Khan IU, Hussain G Journal: Int J Mol Sci (2026) PMID: 42074268 DOI: 10.3390/ijms27083624 Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42074268/ Scraped: 2026-05-05

Abstract

Peripheral nerve injury is a leading cause of disability, which can result in partial or complete loss of motor, sensory, and autonomic function, and currently, there is no effective treatment for this incapacitating condition. It is important to identify new compounds that enable rapid and complete functional recovery. This study evaluated the effects of isorhamnetin (ISO) on functional rehabilitation in a mouse model of sciatic nerve injury.
