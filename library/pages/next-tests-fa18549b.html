Next Tests — BMS-P1 v0.2 NPY–Orexin Stabilization Axis

Literature Checks

  1. NPY receptor subtype effects in anxiety/stress/sleep models: - NPY1R - NPY2R - NPY5R
  2. Bipolar-specific sleep/circadian markers: - sleep deprivation switch risk - actigraphy/circadian rhythm instability - orexin/hypocretin biomarker studies
  3. Orexin antagonist psychiatric safety: - daridorexant, lemborexant, suvorexant literature in depression/bipolar/insomnia populations
  4. Peptide delivery feasibility for CNS: - intranasal NPY literature - BBB penetration strategies - peripheral vs central mechanism distinction

Assay Plan

Stage Test Purpose
1 Receptor subtype map: NPY1R/NPY2R/NPY5R Identify stabilizing vs destabilizing NPY direction
2 HCRTR1/HCRTR2 comparator assay Ensure orexin agonism is excluded
3 Sleep/circadian model Measure sleep timing, fragmentation, and rhythm stability
4 Sleep-deprivation mania model Test anti-manic stabilization, not just sedation
5 DA/glutamate activation screen Kill candidates that look stimulant-like
6 Safety hard-stop screen Seizure, suicidality proxy, serotonergic interaction, adverse behavioral activation

Go / No-Go Criteria

Go

  • Reduces stress/arousal or sleep-deprivation mania-like behavior
  • Stabilizes sleep/circadian readouts
  • Does not increase locomotor hyperactivity
  • Does not show dopamine/glutamate activation pattern
  • Has a defined receptor subtype mechanism

No-Go

  • Wake-promoting orexin agonism
  • Sleep disruption
  • Stimulant-like behavior
  • Neuroplasticity activation without anti-manic guardrail
  • Unclear target
  • Any clinical/self-use implication
